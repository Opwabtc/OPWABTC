2. Nature of the Platform

OPWA Protocol is an experimental, decentralized real estate tokenization protocol built natively on Bitcoin's Layer 1 via OP_NET smart contracts. No mainnet deployment exists at this time. All assets, balances, and transactions are experimental and carry no guarantee of value, liquidity, or real-world legal enforceability. 5. YieldVault & Staking

The YieldVault smart contract allows OPWAY holders to stake tokens in exchange for USDOP stablecoin yield at approximately 15% APY, subject to a 420-block timelock (~70 minutes on OP_NET Testnet). Yield accrual rates, timelock durations, and reward token availability are subject to change via protocol governance. Staked tokens are locked per the contract logic; OPWA Protocol cannot override on-chain conditions or manually release funds. Yield estimates displayed in the interface are approximations and may differ from actual on-chain balances due to block timing variances.

6. PropertyNFT & Property Vault

PropertyNFT (OP-721) tokens represent experimental fractional ownership interests in simulated real estate assets on testnet. These tokens have no legal real-world property rights, title, or enforceability unless explicitly established through applicable law and separate legal documentation. Listing a PropertyNFT in the Property Vault is an on-chain action. OPWA Protocol does not verify, appraise, or warrant any property valuations entered by users. All property data is user-generated and unverified.

Digital assets and DeFi protocols involve substantial risk. Smart contract bugs, oracle failures, network congestion, or protocol exploits may result in partial or total loss of funds. Price volatility of BTC and OPWAY tokens is significant. OP_NET Testnet is an experimental environment and the protocol may be reset, paused, or discontinued without notice. Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026 · OPWA Protocol · OP_NET Testnet

1. Overview & Commitment

OPWA Protocol is built on the principles of transparency, decentralization, and user sovereignty. This Privacy Policy describes what information we collect (or explicitly do not collect), how we operate, and your rights as a user of the OPWA Protocol platform. We are committed to data minimization — we only process what is strictly necessary for the protocol to function.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

OPWA Protocol does not collect, store, or process personally identifiable information (PII) such as your name, email address, phone number, physical address, or government identification. We do not require account registration or login credentials. We do not use tracking cookies, advertising pixels, or behavioral profiling. We do not sell user data to any third party.

3. Blockchain & On-Chain Data

All transactions executed through OPWA Protocol are recorded permanently on the Bitcoin blockchain via OP_NET. This includes wallet addresses, transaction hashes, token transfer amounts, smart contract interactions, NFT minting events, and vault staking actions. This data is publicly visible on OPScan (opscan.org) and inherent to the transparent nature of blockchain technology. OPWA Protocol has no ability to delete, modify, or obscure on-chain data.

4. Wallet Address Usage

When you connect a wallet, OPWA Protocol reads your public key and wallet address solely for the purpose of: displaying your BTC and token balances, loading your transaction history, rendering your portfolio dashboard, and submitting signed transactions on your behalf. Your public key is used to derive your on-chain identity on OP_NET. We never request, store, or transmit your private key or seed phrase.

5. Analytics & Platform Telemetry

We may use privacy-respecting, anonymized analytics to understand aggregate platform usage patterns such as page visit counts, feature usage frequency, and error rates. These analytics do not contain wallet addresses or any user-identifiable data. We do not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or similar tracking services that build behavioral profiles. Any analytics tool used will be disclosed in future updates to this policy.

6. Third-Party Integrations

OPWA Protocol integrates with several external services to provide functionality: CoinGecko API for live BTC price data (no personal data shared); Mempool.opnet.org for OP_NET block data and transaction broadcasting (wallet address included in transactions); OPScan for blockchain explorer links (publicly accessible data); Google Fonts (Syne, DM Sans, DM Mono) loaded via CDN — Google may log IP addresses per their privacy policy. Each third-party service operates under its own privacy policy.

7. Local Storage

OPWA Protocol uses browser localStorage to persist user preferences (theme selection) and vault deposit state (deposit block, OPWAY amount) for UI continuity between sessions. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to OPWA servers. You can clear this data at any time through your browser settings.

8. Children's Privacy

OPWA Protocol is not intended for users under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect any information from minors. If you believe a minor has accessed the platform, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

9. Security

OPWA Protocol employs standard web security practices including HTTPS encryption for all data in transit. However, no system is 100% secure. Given the non-custodial nature of the protocol, the security of your funds ultimately depends on the security of your own wallet and private keys. We strongly recommend using hardware wallets for significant holdings and never reusing seed phrases across services.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as the protocol evolves. Any material changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of OPWA Protocol after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

11. Your Rights

Given the non-custodial and on-chain nature of OPWA Protocol, most data processing is performed transparently on the public blockchain and cannot be altered or deleted. For any off-chain data concerns, you may contact us via GitHub to request clarification, correction of documentation, or erasure of any off-chain records we may hold (if applicable). We will respond within 30 days.

12. Contact

For privacy-related inquiries, contact the OPWA Protocol team via github.com/Opwabtc or through official community channels. Please include "Privacy Inquiry" in your subject line.

OPWA Protocol is designed with your privacy in mind. We believe in building trustless, transparent systems where you remain in full control of your data and assets.
